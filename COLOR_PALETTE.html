Accessibility & Contrast

  • Primary text (#e4e6eb) on dark (#0f1419): 13.5:1 ratio (AAA)
  • Secondary text (#a0a9ba) on dark (#0f1419): 8.2:1 ratio (AAA)
  • Neon colors supplemented with text styling (not color-alone)
  • Focus outlines use high-contrast cyan (#00d9ff)
  • Hover states provide visual feedback without color-only indicators
  • All interactive elements have visible focus styles
Note: While neon colors create visual interest, all UI states are distinguishable through multiple channels (color, shape, text, animation).

Design Rationale

Why Dark Theme?

Dark themes reduce eye strain during extended security analysis sessions and are standard in professional security tools (Burp Suite, OWASP ZAP). The dark background (#0f1419) provides excellent contrast for text and reduces blue light emission.

Why Neon Accents?

High-contrast neon colors (cyan, green, pink) immediately draw attention to critical UI elements and actions. This is familiar to security professionals from tools like Metasploit and creates a sense of professional authority and technical competence.

Why Cyan Primary?

Cyan is universally recognized as a cybersecurity/tech color, suggests scanning/analysis, and provides excellent contrast against dark backgrounds. It's non-aggressive and readable for extended viewing periods.

Why Green for Success?

Green is the universal indicator for safety, success, and completion. Using it for the FREE badge and success states is intuitive and requires no learning curve for users.
